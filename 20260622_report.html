Key Themes

  • **Commodity Market Weakness:** A broad sell-off was observed across crude oil, grains, and softs (cotton, coffee, sugar), with key drivers including receding supply fears (oil) and seasonal weather patterns (coffee, grains).
  • **Weather-Driven Divergence:** Weather played a dual role: excessive rain in the Ivory Coast boosted cocoa prices, while drier weather in Brazil pressured coffee prices. Hotter US weather supported natural gas.
  • **Dollar Strength Pressures Assets:** The US dollar strengthened on euro weakness and higher yields, which typically acts as a headwind for dollar-denominated commodities.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Crude Oil Price Reversal:** The decline was tied to US-Iran peace progress. A breakdown in talks could quickly reverse the trend and spike oil prices.
  • **Weather Uncertainty for Grains:** Corn and soybean bulls are entering the critical growing month of July with weather not on their side, posing a significant supply risk.
  • **Profit-Taking in Cattle:** Cattle prices slipped off session highs after strong gains, indicating a risk of a short-term pullback.
