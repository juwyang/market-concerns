Key Themes

  • **Broad commodity rally:** Grains (corn, soybeans, wheat, cotton) posted strong gains, supported by weather concerns, holiday positioning, and Chinese rumors.
  • **Softs surge on supply risks:** Cocoa and coffee spiked sharply on weather/rain concerns in West Africa and Brazil; sugar gained on currency weakness.
  • **Energy mixed:** Natural gas rose on heatwave demand, while crude eased on global supply rebound; gasoline surged.
  • **Livestock uncertain:** Hogs and cattle ended mixed with thin trade; cattle faced collapse risk while pork demand outlook offered support.
  • **Dollar slightly lower:** The dollar failed to hold early gains after a weak payroll report overshadowed services data.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Weak export data for wheat** could cap grain rallies if support from corn and soybeans fades.
  • **Cattle collapse risk** flagged in the market could pressure live cattle futures.
  • **Volatility in crude oil** as supply rebounds and gasoline strength creates a mixed picture.
  • **Potential reversal in softs** if weather conditions improve (cocoa/coffee) or currency trends shift (sugar).

Watch Next

  • **U.S. weather forecasts** for corn and soybean growing regions (warmer/dryer scenario).
  • **Chinese buying rumors** for soybeans – any confirmation or denial could shift prices.
  • **USDA reports and export inspections** – especially for wheat and corn.
  • **Fed rate policy cues** – follow-up on payroll and ISM services data for the dollar.
