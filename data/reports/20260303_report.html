ENERGY

Crude Oil & RBOB Gasoline

+4.67%
Rose sharply on Tuesday. April WTI crude oil closed up +3.33 (+4.67%) and April RBOB gasoline closed up +0.0868 (+3.66%). Prices were up significantly earlier in the day.

Key Drivers

  • Supply disruptions from the closure of the Strait of Hormuz to tanker traffic due to war in Iran. Soaring oil prices are boosting inflation expectations.

Reverse Factors

  • The geopolitical situation is fluid; any resolution or easing of tensions in the Strait of Hormuz could rapidly reverse price gains.
`Short-term influencer - UP`

+0.61%

Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical Risk Drives Energy:** The closure of the Strait of Hormuz due to conflict in Iran triggered a sharp, multi-day rally in crude oil and related energy products.
  • **Broad Commodity Impact:** The surge in oil prices had ripple effects, boosting inflation expectations (supporting the dollar, pressuring gold), and providing lift to biofuel-linked commodities like sugar.
  • **Mixed Performances Elsewhere:** Grains and livestock markets showed mixed or steady action, with some (soybeans, corn, cattle) finding late strength, while others (wheat, hogs) were directionless.
  • **Currency as a Key Driver:** Strength in the U.S. Dollar and weakness in the Brazilian Real were cited as direct price drivers for commodities like cotton and coffee.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Geopolitical Supply Disruption:** The war in Iran has closed a critical oil chokepoint (Strait of Hormuz), disrupting global supply.
  • **Inflation Resurgence:** Soaring energy prices are boosting inflation expectations, which may alter central bank policy timelines.
  • **Demand Uncertainty:** Articles mention "uncertainty with China" as a factor weighing on cotton markets.
  • **Currency Volatility:** Fluctuations in the U.S. Dollar and the Brazilian Real are directly impacting commodity prices.
