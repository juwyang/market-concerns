Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical risk dominates:** The Iran war and Middle East tensions are the primary drivers for crude oil, gasoline, cocoa, coffee, and the dollar.
  • **Softs rally on supply fears:** Cocoa and coffee prices surged due to war-related supply disruption concerns; sugar benefited from crude oil and currency support.
  • **Grains mixed:** Wheat rallied without a clear fundamental trigger, soybeans softened, and corn recovered to close higher on possible positive export sales.
  • **Livestock rebounds:** Hogs and cattle both closed higher after recent weakness, driven by technical factors and steady cash trade.

  • **Natural gas oversupply:** Growing U.S. storage levels continue to pressure prices lower, a trend that may persist if storage builds remain strong.
  • **Cotton topping risk:** One article warns cotton prices have peaked and expects further weakness, presenting a bearish counter-narrative.

Watch Next
