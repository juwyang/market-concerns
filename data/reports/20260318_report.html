Key Themes

  • **Broad Commodity Strength:** Grains (wheat, soybeans, corn) rallied significantly, while energy and softs like sugar were lifted by geopolitical and fuel market dynamics.
  • **Geopolitical Risk Premium:** Middle East tensions, specifically threats from Iran, provided a bid to oil, natural gas, and supported the dollar as a safe-haven.
  • **Inflation & Fed Focus:** A hotter-than-expected U.S. PPI report strengthened the dollar, pressured metals, and kept market attention firmly on Federal Reserve policy.
  • **Idiosyncratic Events:** Specific supply-side issues drove action: a meatpacking strike impacted cattle, crop outlooks weighed on cocoa and coffee, and regulatory news boosted OKLO.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Geopolitical Escalation:** Iran's threats to Middle Eastern energy infrastructure pose a significant risk to oil and gas supply and prices.
  • **Economic Slowdown:** Goldman Sachs issued a warning on the U.S. economy, cutting its growth forecast and raising the probability of a recession.
  • **Supply Chain Disruption:** A major strike at a JBS meatpacking plant is disrupting U.S. beef production, creating opposing pressures for producer and consumer prices.
  • **Persistent Inflation:** Strong PPI data suggests ongoing inflationary pressures, which could compel the Fed to maintain a hawkish stance longer than markets expect.

Watch Next

  • Commentary from Fed Chair Jerome Powell's press conference.
