Key Themes

  • **Energy weakness:** Crude oil and gasoline fell sharply on expectations of normalized global supplies after the end of the US-Iran war.
  • **Weather driving softs:** Cocoa, sugar, and coffee rallied on weather-related supply concerns (El Niño, Indian monsoon, Brazil harvest delays).
  • **Mixed grains:** Soybeans gained on Chinese inquiry rumors, while wheat and corn saw mixed trade with Chicago wheat leading.
  • **Livestock divergence:** Cattle rallied strongly, while hogs showed weakness in front-month contracts.
  • **Dollar under pressure:** Weak US housing data and lower crude oil contributed to a softer dollar.

Risks Highlighted

  • **El Niño weather risks:** Potential for prolonged impact on cocoa production, but also risk of exaggerated pricing.
  • **Indian monsoon deficiency:** Slack rains could further tighten sugar supplies, but improvement could reverse gains.
  • **Brazil coffee harvest delays:** Rain delays are temporary; a rapid harvest catch-up could pressure prices.
  • **US housing weakness:** Soft housing starts and permits may signal economic slowdown, weighing on the dollar.
  • **Crude supply normalization:** The end of the US-Iran war may boost oil supplies, but any geopolitical setback could reverse.
