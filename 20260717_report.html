Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical Tension Fuels Energy Rally:** The escalation of US-Iran hostilities was the dominant theme, driving a sharp rally in crude oil and gasoline prices, which in turn supported natural gas and sugar.
  • **Mixed Performance in Grains and Ags:** Wheat, soybeans, and corn managed to close higher for the week, while livestock was mixed with hogs gaining and cattle declining.
  • **Demand and Supply Dynamics in Softs:** Coffee and cocoa prices were lifted by supply concerns (Brazil harvest delay) and demand surprises (North American cocoa demand), respectively.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Geopolitical Escalation/Risk Reversal:** The sharp rally in crude oil is directly tied to US-Iran hostilities. Any de-escalation could lead to a rapid price reversal.
  • **Hawkish Fed Stance:** Surging crude oil prices raise inflation expectations, which could prompt the Federal Reserve to pursue tighter monetary policy, impacting the dollar and broader markets.
  • **Weaker Cash Markets in Livestock:** Declining cash trade and beef prices were cited as a key driver for the slide in live cattle futures, signaling a bearish fundamental headwind.

Watch Next

  • **US-Iran Hostilities:** The next developments in the geopolitical conflict will be critical for energy prices and related markets.
  • **Export Sales Data:** The recent rally in soybeans was attributed to a "slew of export business," making future export data a key indicator to watch.
  • **Brazil Coffee Harvest Progress:** The speed of Brazil’s coffee harvest will be a determining factor for coffee price direction.
