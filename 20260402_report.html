Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical Dominance:** The war in Iran and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz are the overwhelming market drivers, causing a historic surge in energy prices and rippling through other asset classes.
  • **Agricultural Divergence:** Grain markets showed mixed, muted movements at the week's end, while soft commodities (sugar, cocoa, coffee) fell due to specific supply/demand factors.
  • **Livestock Split:** Cattle rallied on strong cash markets, but the sector faces a macro threat from rising energy-driven input costs. Hogs are under distinct downward pressure.
  • **Safe-Haven Flows:** The US dollar strengthened due to risk-off sentiment and demand for liquidity amid equity market declines and geopolitical uncertainty.
  • **Commodity-Specific Fundamentals:** Outside of geopolitics, individual commodity markets are responding to factors like Indian sugar production, Brazilian coffee crops, and Easter chocolate demand.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Prolonged Iran Conflict:** The continued closure of the Strait of Hormuz risks sustaining high energy prices and broader market volatility.
  • **Consumer Demand Erosion:** Rising energy and input prices threaten to shrink consumer disposable income, potentially breaking the rally in markets like cattle.
  • **Supply Gluts:** Increased production, as seen with sugar in India and natural gas storage in the US, is applying downward price pressure in those markets.
  • **Weak Seasonal Demand:** Soft demand for Easter chocolate is cited as a specific risk for cocoa prices.
