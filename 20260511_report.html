CURRENCIES & STOCKS

US Dollar Index (DXY)

+0.05%
Rose by +0.05% on Monday, though it erased early gains.

Key Drivers

  • Safe-haven demand after US-Iran talks failed; strength from crude oil. Early support faded due to weaker-than-expected US existing home sales and strength in the Chinese yuan.

Reverse Factors

  • Stock market rally could further pressure the dollar; upcoming economic data.
Short-term influencer - UP

Key Themes

  • **Energy Surge:** The Strait of Hormuz closure drove crude oil and gasoline prices sharply higher, with natural gas also rallying on hot US weather forecasts.
  • **Soft Commodity Rally:** Cocoa, coffee, and sugar all posted strong gains due to supply concerns (El Niño, tight inventories, global supply worries).
  • **Grain Market Uptick:** Wheat rose on deteriorating crop conditions; corn, soybeans, and soybean meal/oil gained broadly, partly supported by crude oil strength.
  • **Livestock Mixed:** Hogs rallied despite pseudorabies concerns, while cattle fell under tariff pressure and high gas prices.
  • **Dollar Edges Higher:** Safe-haven demand lifted the dollar, though gains were capped by weak housing data and yuan strength.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Strait of Hormuz Closure:** Major supply risk for crude and gasoline; could escalate if prolonged.
  • **Deteriorating Wheat Crop Conditions:** May lead to reduced yields and tighter supplies.
  • **El Niño Fears for Cocoa:** Threatens production in West Africa.
  • **Tight Coffee Inventories:** Could cause price spikes if demand remains robust.
  • **Pseudorabies Outbreak in Hogs:** Supply disruption but may also hurt demand.
  • **High Gas Prices Weighing on Cattle:** Increases production costs and reduces demand.
  • **Tariff Pressure on Cattle:** Trade war uncertainty dampens market sentiment.
  • **Weak US Existing Home Sales:** Signals economic softness, could pressure the dollar.

Watch Next

  • **This Week's Trump-Xi Meeting:** Could shift trade dynamics for livestock and grains.
  • **Seasonal Pattern Change in Grains:** After the second weekly close of May, seasonal trends may shift.
  • **Further Crop Condition Reports:** For wheat and other grains.
  • **Developments in the Strait of Hormuz:** Any reopening or further escalation.
  • **US Weather Forecasts:** Potential impact on natural gas and grains.
