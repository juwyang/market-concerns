ENERGY

Crude Oil

-0.25%
Declined. July WTI crude oil closed down -0.19 (-0.25%) on Thursday. Intraday it tumbled to a 3.5-month low on news that the Strait of Hormuz reopened.

Key Drivers

  • US-Iran peace deal and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, which eased supply disruption fears. Crude oil weakness also pressured sugar and cotton prices.

Reverse Factors

  • July RBOB gasoline closed up +0.0853 (+2.93%), showing strength in the refined product market which could support crude. The peace deal's permanence is not guaranteed.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

  • Weakness into the long weekend. Profit-taking and positioning ahead of the US holiday.

Reverse Factors

Soybeans

Declined. Soybeans posted losses of 6 ½ to 9 ¼ cents across the board on Thursday. July held for a 9 ¼ cent weekly gain. The national average Cash Bean price was down 8 1/2 cents at $10.71.

Reverse Factors

Key Themes

  • **Dominant Dollar Strength:** The US dollar rallied to a 13-month high on the back of a hawkish Fed policy update, which was a key driver pressuring gold, sugar, coffee, and cotton prices.
  • **Broad Commodity Weakness:** Most commodity markets (corn, soybeans, wheat, cotton, sugar, crude oil) saw declines on Thursday, partially attributed to profit-taking and positioning ahead of the US long weekend (Friday holiday).
  • **Geopolitical Impact on Energy:** Crude oil prices fell due to a US-Iran peace deal and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, easing supply disruption fears.
  • **Mixed Signals in Livestock:** Hogs and cattle posted mixed results, with front-month strength contrasting with deferred-month weakness and failed cattle auctions.

Risks Highlighted

  • Continued hawkish stance from the Fed leading to further dollar appreciation and sustained pressure on commodity prices.
  • The durability of the US-Iran peace deal and stability in the Strait of Hormuz, as any reversal could spike crude oil prices again.
  • The end of the 2026 soybean rally, as new-crop futures have “run out of upside momentum” and are approaching a test of support.

Watch Next

  • Impact of the US holiday (Friday) on trading volumes and potential catch-up movements when markets reopen.
  • Further clarity on the Fed’s interest rate path and its effect on the dollar and gold.
