CURRENCIES & STOCKS

US Dollar

+0.17%
The dollar index (DXY00) rose by +0.17%, recovering from a 5-week low.

Key Drivers

  • The gain was prompted by positive US labor market news, specifically an unexpected drop in weekly initial unemployment claims to a 3-year low. Higher T-Note yields also provided support.

Reverse Factors

  • The market's focus remains on US labor data, which can be volatile week-to-week.
Short-term influencer - UP

Key Themes

  • **Dollar Strength:** The US dollar rebounded on positive jobless claims data, influencing several dollar-denominated commodities.
  • **Weather-Driven Moves:** Cold US temperatures supported natural gas, while dry Brazilian weather forecasts boosted arabica coffee.
  • **Commodity-Specific Fundamentals:** Export news supported corn, cash markets drove livestock, and Indian production data weighed on sugar.
  • **Geopolitical Undercurrents:** Ongoing tensions provided a floor for crude oil prices.

Risks Highlighted

  • Geopolitical tensions, a current support for crude oil, remain a volatile and unpredictable risk factor.
  • Weather patterns, currently driving natural gas and coffee prices, are subject to rapid change.
  • The strength in the US dollar, if sustained, could continue to pressure commodities priced in dollars, like cocoa.

Watch Next

  • Further developments in US labor market data.
  • Weather forecasts for key agricultural regions (US for nat-gas, Brazil for coffee).
  • Geopolitical developments affecting energy supply.
