ENERGY

Crude Oil

-5.55%
July WTI crude oil closed sharply lower, down -$5.21 (-5.55%). July RBOB gasoline also fell -0.0798 (-2.54%).

Key Drivers

  • Optimism over a potential US-Iran peace deal and continued talks on reopening the Hormuz Strait pressured prices. Crude oil losses extended from Tuesday, with a second day of sharp declines.

Reverse Factors

  • If peace talks stall or break down, supply fears could push prices higher. Profit-taking after the steep drop is also possible.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

Natural Gas

+5.04%

Key Drivers

Reverse Factors

  • Should weather forecasts cool again, demand expectations could reverse, leading to price declines.
Short-term influencer - UP

CURRENCIES & STOCKS

US Dollar Index

+0.03%
The dollar index (DXY00) rose by +0.03% on Wednesday, recovering from early losses.

Key Drivers

  • Conflicting signals about the prospects for a US-Iran peace plan boosted the dollar. Earlier pressure from crude oil's plunge faded.

Reverse Factors

  • A clear breakthrough in US-Iran talks could remove safe-haven demand for the dollar, while a breakdown could further strengthen it.
Short-term influencer - UP

Key Themes

  • **Crude Oil Plunge Dominates:** A sharp drop in crude oil (driven by US-Iran peace optimism and Hormuz reopening talks) spilled over into grains (wheat, corn) and softs (cotton), weighing on those commodities.
  • **Weather Drives Natural Gas Rally:** Warmer US weather forecasts lifted natural gas prices sharply, reversing earlier weakness.
  • **Dollar Strength Impacts Softs:** A recovering US dollar pressured cocoa, cotton, and contributed to losses in crude-linked commodities.
  • **Brazil Supply Overhang:** Higher Brazilian sucrose yields and increased sugar production pushed sugar prices lower.
  • **Currency Weakness Hits Coffee:** The Brazilian real's slide undercut coffee prices, encouraging producer selling.
  • **Livestock Technical Rebounds:** Hogs and cattle rallied on no clear fundamental news, suggesting short-term technical moves.

Risks Highlighted

  • **US-Iran Peace Uncertainty:** Conflicting signals on the peace deal create volatility for crude oil and the dollar. A breakdown could reverse the sharp crude decline.
  • **Weather Forecast Reversals:** Natural gas is highly sensitive to weather model changes; a cooling trend could erase today's gains.
  • **Brazilian Real Volatility:** Further weakness in the real could continue to pressure coffee and sugar prices.
  • **Limited Cash Trade in Cattle:** The rally in live cattle lacks cash market confirmation, raising the risk of a correction once trade resumes.
  • **Crude Oil Spillover Risk:** Continued weakness in crude oil could drag down grains and other energy-sensitive commodities further.

Watch Next

  • **NASS Crop Progress Data:** Mentioned for corn and cotton – upcoming reports could influence grain and cotton prices.
  • **Fed Cattle Exchange Auction:** The Wednesday online auction will provide fresh cash cattle price indications.
  • **US Weather Forecasts:** Updates will determine the direction of natural gas prices.
  • **US-Iran Peace Plan Developments:** Any progress or breakdown will directly impact crude oil and the dollar.
