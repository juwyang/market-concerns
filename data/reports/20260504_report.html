Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical risk dominates energy markets:** Escalating Iran tensions drove crude oil and gasoline sharply higher, and also lifted the US dollar as a safe haven.
  • **Grain markets mostly firmer but mixed:** Corn rallied on export demand, soybeans gained without a clear catalyst, and wheat was mixed. A general bullish tone persists from January lows.
  • **Soft commodities show divergence:** Sugar and cocoa surged (gasoline linkage and supply fears), while coffee fell on dollar strength and cotton slipped amid dollar headwinds.
  • **Livestock futures weakened despite supportive cash markets:** Hogs and cattle both declined, with consumer demand concerns from rising gasoline prices flagged as a potential headwind.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Escalation of Iran tensions:** Further flare-ups could continue to boost safe-haven demand and energy prices, but also risk broader macroeconomic uncertainty.
  • **$5 gasoline threat to consumer spending:** High gasoline prices could dent consumer confidence and reduce demand for higher-priced beef (Article 20) and potentially other protein.
  • **Dollar strength pressuring commodities:** A rising dollar may continue to weigh on dollar-denominated commodity prices, particularly for cotton, coffee, and others.

Watch Next

  • **Iran tensions (geopolitical developments):** Any news on negotiations or further military actions will likely impact crude oil, the dollar, and safe-haven assets.
  • **NASS Crop Progress data:** The articles mention pending crop progress updates for corn and cotton; these could shift grain and softs sentiment.
  • **USDA cash and index prices:** Daily hog and cattle cash price movements may provide direction for livestock futures.
