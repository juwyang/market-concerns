Key Themes

  • **Broad Commodity Weakness:** Energy (crude, nat-gas), grains (wheat, soybeans, corn), and coffee saw notable declines.
  • **Dollar-Driven Pressure:** A stronger US dollar, fueled by rising Treasury yields, was cited as a headwind for crude oil and gold.
  • **Weather Sensitivity:** Natural gas prices plunged due to forecasts for warmer US weather, highlighting the market's immediate sensitivity to short-term demand shifts.
  • **Anticipation of Key Data:** Grain markets were described as "wobbly" and trading cautiously ahead of a significant USDA report.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Energy Demand Concerns:** Falling gasoline prices to multi-month lows point to potential weakness in energy consumption.
  • **Weather Volatility:** Natural gas markets remain highly vulnerable to changes in temperature forecasts.
  • **Supply Gluts:** Soft commodities like sugar and coffee face downward pressure from expectations of abundant global supplies.
  • **Government Data Impact:** Grain markets are positioned for potential high volatility following the release of USDA data.

Watch Next

  • The Tuesday USDA report, which grain traders were squaring up ahead of.
  • US weather forecasts for mid-month, which will continue to drive natural gas demand expectations.
  • Developments in Indian sugar production, a key driver for global sugar prices.
