Key Themes

  • **Broad Weakness in Grains & Livestock:** Corn, soybeans, wheat, hogs, and cattle all fell on Thursday, but no specific drivers were cited in the articles, suggesting a general risk-off or technical sell-off.
  • **Energy Declines on Geopolitical Hopes:** Crude oil and gasoline erased early gains to close lower, driven by optimism for a US-Iran peace deal that could reopen the Strait of Hormuz.
  • **Mixed Softs on Weather and Dollar:** Sugar and coffee rallied on weather concerns (dry conditions and El Niño), while cocoa fell under pressure from a strong dollar and rising inventories.
  • **Dollar Strengthened by Middle East Uncertainty:** The dollar hit a 6-week high as safe-haven demand increased amid doubts over the Iran peace process.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Middle East Uncertainty:** Doubts over the US-Iran peace deal and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz continue to drive volatility in crude oil and the dollar.
  • **El Niño Weather Pattern:** Potential disruption to coffee (and possibly other softs) if an El Niño develops.
  • **Rising ICE Cocoa Inventories:** Increasing cocoa stockpiles are weighing on prices and could persist.
  • **Dry Weather for Sugar:** Ongoing dry conditions in key growing areas could threaten sugar supply.

Watch Next

  • **US-Iran Peace Deal Progress:** Any news on the Strait of Hormuz reopening will directly impact crude oil, gasoline, and the dollar.
  • **El Niño Developments:** Weather forecasts and official models will be critical for coffee and other weather-sensitive softs.
  • **Weekly Cocoa Inventory Data:** Further rises in ICE cocoa stocks could push prices lower.
