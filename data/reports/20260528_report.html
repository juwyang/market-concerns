Key Themes

  • **Divergent Commodity Performance:** Energy and most grains saw gains, while a few softs (cocoa, sugar) and livestock (cattle) declined.
  • **Geopolitical Influence on Energy:** Crude oil prices were supported by hopes for a US-Iran peace deal being not yet realized and the continued closure of the Strait of Hormuz.
  • **Supply and Weather as Key Short-Term Drivers:** Price movements in softs were heavily influenced by supply data (cocoa inventories, Brazilian sugar production) and weather (rains delaying Brazil's coffee harvest, hotter US weather boosting natural gas).
  • **US Dollar Weakness:** The dollar index declined on disappointing US economic data, which often provides support for dollar-denominated commodities like cotton and crude oil.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Geopolitical Reversal:** A realized US-Iran peace deal could remove a key support factor for crude oil prices.
  • **Profit-Taking After Rallies:** Cattle's decline on Thursday followed a significant Wednesday rally, highlighting the risk of pullbacks after strong moves.
  • **Conflicting Price Signals:** Contradictory intra-day data (e.g., early losses in cotton followed by gains) suggests market sentiment can be volatile.

Watch Next

  • **EIA Data:** The article on corn mentioned EIA data from the morning, the details of which will be important for the energy and grains outlook.
  • **Cash Trade Volume:** For cattle and hogs, the volume and price levels of cash trade will be a key market indicator to watch for direction.
  • **US Economic News:** The dollar's decline was triggered by weaker-than-expected US economic news; further releases could impact both the dollar and commodity markets.
