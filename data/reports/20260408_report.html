Date: 2026-04-08

ENERGY

Crude Oil (WTI) & RBOB Gasoline

16.41%
Plummeted sharply. May WTI crude closed down 16.41% (-$18.54) and May RBOB gasoline closed down 9.06% (-$0.2993).

Key Drivers

  • The US and Iran agreed to a ceasefire, significantly easing geopolitical risks and reducing the war-risk premium in oil prices.

Reverse Factors

  • Any resurgence of geopolitical tensions or supply disruptions could reverse the price decline. The sharp sell-off may also lead to technical rebounds or profit-taking.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

Natural Gas

5.09%
Tumbled sharply, settling down 5.09% (-$0.146) to a 7.5-month low.

Key Drivers

  • Above-normal US weather forecasts reducing heating demand, coupled with sinking crude oil prices which pressured the broader energy complex.

Reverse Factors

  • A shift to colder-than-expected weather patterns or a sustained recovery in crude oil prices could provide support.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

GRAINS

Wheat (Chicago SRW & KC HRW)

Collapsed under significant pressure. Chicago SRW futures were down 13 to 19 cents.

Key Drivers

  • Heavy selling pressure linked to the sharp drop in crude oil prices, as money flowed out of commodities.

Reverse Factors

  • A rebound in energy markets or fresh concerns over global wheat supply (e.g., weather issues) could stabilize or lift prices.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

Corn

Recovered off early lows but still closed in the red, down 1 ¾ to 5 cents. The national average cash price was lower.

Reverse Factors

  • Continued weakness in crude oil could maintain downward pressure, while stronger-than-expected demand or supportive crop data could help prices recover.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

Soybeans

Reverse Factors

  • If meal support wanes or if broader commodity selling intensifies, gains could be reversed.

CURRENCIES & STOCKS

US Dollar Index

0.71%
Fell sharply, down 0.71% to 1.13% on the day, hitting a 4-week low.

Key Drivers

  • The US-Iran ceasefire agreement curbed safe-haven demand for the dollar.

Reverse Factors

  • Any breakdown in the ceasefire or renewed global risk aversion could see investors flock back to the dollar.
Short-term influencer - DOWN

Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical De-escalation Drives Markets:** The announcement of a US-Iran ceasefire was the dominant market-moving event, triggering a massive risk-off repositioning.
  • **Energy Complex Crumbles:** Crude oil, gasoline, and natural gas prices fell sharply as the geopolitical risk premium evaporated and warm weather forecasts added pressure.
  • **Dollar Weakness Boosts Commodities:** The plunge in the dollar provided a significant lift to dollar-denominated commodities, most notably gold and cocoa.
  • **Agricultural Markets React Divergently:** While wheat and corn were pulled lower by the oil sell-off, soybeans found support from meal, and softs were mixed, responding to both currency moves and energy prices.

Risks Highlighted

  • Geopolitical risk resuming if the US-Iran ceasefire breaks down.
  • Light trading volume and data availability, as seen in the hog market, leading to heightened volatility.
  • Continued pressure on agricultural commodities if energy prices remain weak.
  • Currency volatility, particularly in the Brazilian Real, impacting soft commodity exports.

Watch Next

  • Development of cash trade for live cattle.
  • USDA reports on national base prices and lean hog indices.
  • Further developments regarding the US-Iran ceasefire and broader geopolitical stability.
  • Weather forecasts and their impact on US natural gas demand and crop conditions.
  • Movements in the Brazilian Real and its continued influence on coffee and sugar markets.
