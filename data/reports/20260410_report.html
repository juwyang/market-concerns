US Dollar Index

0.15%
The dollar index (DXY) fell, down 0.15% on Friday and 0.26% earlier in the day.

Key Drivers

  • Weaker-than-expected US economic news, including a smaller-than-expected increase in March consumer prices and a plunge in the University of Michigan’s consumer sentiment index. A stock market rally also reduced safe-haven demand.

Reverse Factors

  • Stronger economic data or a shift to risk-off sentiment could strengthen the dollar.
`Short-term influencer - DOWN` (Driven by immediate economic data and market sentiment.)
