Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical conflict drives energy rally:** The ongoing US-Iran war and threat to the Strait of Hormuz pushed crude oil and gasoline sharply higher, with spillover effects on sugar.
  • **Month-end profit taking in grains and cotton:** Wheat and cotton saw profit-taking pressure after early gains, while corn and soybeans held modestly higher.
  • **Strong US data and hawkish Fed dissents boost dollar:** Better-than-expected housing and capital goods orders, plus three hawkish FOMC dissents, supported the dollar.
  • **Soft commodities mixed:** Cocoa rose on smaller surplus outlook, coffee fell on dollar strength, sugar surged on crude link.
  • **Livestock rallies on cash trade:** Hog and cattle prices benefited from higher cash prices, particularly front-month contracts.
  • **Earnings-driven stock surges:** Bloom Energy and Archrock hit all-time highs on strong earnings and demand outlook.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Prolonged US-Iran war risk to global energy supply and shipping lanes** (e.g., Hormuz closure).
  • **Month-end profit taking pressure** could reverse rallies in multiple commodities.
  • **Dollar strength** continues to weigh on dollar-denominated softs like coffee.
  • **Abundant US natural gas storage** risks further downside if storage builds persist.
  • **Back-month weakness in soybeans and cattle** suggests caution in sustained rallies.

Watch Next

  • **FOMC meeting outcome and any dissents** (mentioned in multiple articles).
  • **US-Iran conflict developments** for further crude oil and gasoline volatility.
  • **End-of-month positioning** across all commodities, especially cotton and wheat.
  • **Weekly EIA storage report** for natural gas (noted in corn article).
  • **Earnings reports** from energy and industrial sectors (following Bloom Energy and Archrock).
