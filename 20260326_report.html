Key Themes

  • Geopolitical tension surrounding the Iran war was the dominant market theme, driving safe-haven flows into the US dollar and sparking a sharp rally in crude oil.
  • Strength in crude oil had a ripple effect, supporting prices in linked commodities like sugar.
  • Most agricultural and soft commodities (wheat, cattle, hogs, cotton, sugar, cocoa) saw gains driven by short-term factors like technical moves, session dynamics, or the oil spillover.
  • Coffee was a notable exception, falling due to the long-term bearish outlook of a record Brazilian crop.

Risks Highlighted

  • Escalation of the Iran war, disrupting oil supply and broader market stability.
  • Geopolitical uncertainty failing to resolve, keeping safe-haven demand and oil volatility elevated.
  • Weather and crop conditions, particularly favorable outlooks in West Africa (cocoa) and Brazil (coffee), applying long-term price pressure.

Watch Next

  • Developments regarding a potential ceasefire in Iran.
  • Weekly EIA inventory reports for energy products.
  • Ongoing forecasts and reports on crop conditions in key producing regions like Brazil and West Africa.
