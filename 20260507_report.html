Key Themes

  • Commodity markets broadly declined, with grains, livestock, cotton, crude oil, coffee, and sugar all falling.
  • Cocoa was a notable exception, surging on El Niño-driven supply fears.
  • The US dollar recovered on stock market weakness, adding pressure to dollar-denominated commodities.
  • Iran ceasefire rumors continued to weigh on crude oil and support the dollar’s earlier dip.
  • Speculative liquidation and false headlines were cited as drivers of the grain sell-off (Article 13).

Risks Highlighted

  • Iran ceasefire rumors may be false, creating a potential “commodity trap” as speculative liquidation reverses (Article 13).
  • El Niño could further disrupt cocoa supply, fueling additional price spikes.
  • A bumper Brazil coffee crop may keep arabica prices under pressure.
  • Ethanol weakness could lead to increased sugar production, sustaining downside for sugar.
