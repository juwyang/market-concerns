Key Themes

  • Commodity markets were dominated by short-term, news-driven movements.
  • Energy and grains (soybeans, corn, wheat) sold off sharply, largely on eased supply fears (Strait of Hormuz) and demand uncertainty (China).
  • Livestock (cattle) and several softs (cotton, cocoa, coffee) rallied on specific catalysts like strong cash trade, China sentiment, dollar weakness, and supply concerns.
  • Currency markets saw the dollar weaken in tandem with a rebound in equities and lower bond yields.

Risks Highlighted

  • Uncertainty surrounding Chinese demand, specifically for soybeans and cotton.
  • Geopolitical and logistical risks affecting commodity supply chains (Strait of Hormuz for oil, unspecified disruptions for coffee).
  • Market volatility, highlighted by expanded trading limits for soybeans following a limit-down move.

Watch Next
