ENERGY

Crude Oil & Gasoline

-0.26%
Prices settled mixed on Monday, with April WTI crude oil closing down $0.17 (-0.26%). Early gains were erased. Gasoline (RBJ26) closed up slightly (+0.16%). Earlier in the day, prices had moved higher with crude climbing to a 6.5-month high.

Key Drivers

  • Early gains were attributed to geopolitical risks. Later, prices gave up gains and settled mixed on energy demand concerns.

Reverse Factors

  • The balance between geopolitical risk support and demand concerns could shift. An escalation in risk or improvement in demand outlook could push prices higher, while easing tensions or worsening demand fears could lead to further declines.
`Unclear` (Conflicting drivers of short-term geopolitical risk and demand concerns)

Natural Gas

-2.03%
March Nymex natural gas closed down by $0.062 (-2.03%), erasing early gains.

Key Drivers

  • Updated US weather forecasts called for above-normal temperatures, reducing heating demand.

Reverse Factors

  • A shift back to colder-than-expected weather forecasts could reverse the decline.
`Short-term influencer - DOWN` (Weather forecast change)

GRAINS

Wheat

All wheat markets closed lower on Monday to start the week, failing to extend prior gains. Chicago SRW futures were down 4 to 7 cents, and KC HRW was down 12 to 13 cents.

Key Drivers

  • The market is facing uncertainty over potential US tariff regimes. Earlier in the week, prices were set up for chart-based buying.

Reverse Factors

  • Clarification or easing of trade/tariff uncertainty could support prices. Chart-based buying momentum mentioned earlier in the week could re-emerge.
`Unclear` (Conflict between potential technical buying and trade policy uncertainty)

Corn

Corn futures closed steady to fractionally higher across most contracts on Monday. Prices were up 1 to 3 cents at midday.

Key Drivers

  • The market received spillover support from other grains and a private export sale reported by USDA. The national average cash price was steady to slightly higher.

Reverse Factors

  • A lack of follow-through export sales or a downturn in supporting grain markets could pressure prices.
`Short-term influencer - UP` (Spillover support and export sale)

Soybeans

Soybeans closed with losses of 2 to 6 cents in the nearby contracts on Monday.

Key Drivers

  • The national average cash bean price was down. Some deferred contracts held up better.

Reverse Factors

  • Strength in deferred contracts suggests underlying support; a positive shift in demand news could reverse losses.
`Short-term influencer - DOWN` (Cash market weakness)

LIVESTOCK

Lean Hogs

Lean hog futures closed with gains of a tick to 50 cents on Monday, continuing a bounce from the prior week.

Key Drivers

  • USDA’s national base hog price was reported at $91.28, up $2.32 from the day prior.

Reverse Factors

  • Historically, hog prices face seasonal pressure after February as slaughter and pork supply increase.
`Short-term influencer - UP` (Daily cash price increase)

Reverse Factors

`Short-term influencer - DOWN` (Limited cash trade activity)

CURRENCIES & STOCKS

US Dollar

0.10%
The US Dollar Index (DXY) fell on Monday, down 0.10% to 0.25%.

Key Drivers

  • Concern that foreign investors may shy away from dollar assets after President Trump signed an executive order related to trade, creating policy uncertainty.

Reverse Factors

  • Clarification on trade policy or a shift in investor sentiment could strengthen the dollar.
`Short-term influencer - DOWN` (Trade policy uncertainty)

Key Themes

  • **Broad Commodity Weakness:** Many agricultural commodities (wheat, soybeans, cotton, cattle) and energy (natural gas) closed lower on Monday.
  • **Trade Policy Uncertainty:** Concerns over potential US tariff regimes were cited as a key factor pressuring the dollar and creating uncertainty in wheat markets.
  • **Weather-Driven Moves:** Natural gas fell specifically due to forecasts for warmer US weather, while agricultural markets monitor conditions.
  • **Softs Divergence:** Soft commodities showed split action, with sugar rising on currency strength while cocoa and coffee fell on fundamental supply prospects.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Trade Policy Risk:** Uncertainty over the new US administration's tariff regime could deter foreign investment in dollar assets and disrupt agricultural markets.
  • **Demand Concerns:** Fears about energy demand contributed to pulling crude oil prices off their highs.
  • **Supply Glut:** Cocoa and coffee markets are under pressure from expectations of robust or bumper supplies.
  • **Seasonal Pressure:** Lean hog markets historically face a seasonal price decline after February.

Watch Next

  • **US Trade Policy Developments:** Further details or market reaction to the recent executive order on trade.
  • **US Weather Forecasts:** Continued updates, particularly for natural gas demand and growing conditions for grains.
  • **USDA Export Data:** Further private export sales or weekly reports, which provided support to corn.
