Key Themes

  • **Broad Commodity Rally:** A wide range of commodities, including crude oil, wheat, corn, cattle, cotton, sugar, cocoa, and coffee, posted gains on Tuesday.
  • **Supply Constraints as a Key Driver:** Tighter supplies due to geopolitical factors (Strait of Hormuz closure for crude oil) and production issues (smaller Brazil sugar output, supply fears for cocoa, shrinking coffee inventories) were major upward catalysts.
  • **Divergent Performance in Grains & Livestock:** Wheat and corn rallied, while soybeans closed mixed. In livestock, cattle rallied on cash strength, but hogs slipped lower.
  • **Dollar Strength Amid Stock Weakness:** The US dollar advanced, supported by a stock market slump and rising crude oil prices, which boosted its safe-haven and inflation-hedge appeal.

Risks Highlighted

  • **Geopolitical Supply Risks:** The closure of the Strait of Hormuz is underpinning crude oil and gasoline prices, posing a significant risk to global energy supply stability.
  • **Long-Term Demand Shifts:** Changing consumer tastes are permanently altering demand for orange juice and potentially cotton, signaling structural headwinds for these softs.
  • **Weather-Dependent Demand:** Below-normal spring temperatures are supporting natural gas prices, but this driver is highly weather-dependent and could reverse.

Watch Next

  • **Oil Supply Developments:** Monitor any news regarding the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz or shifts in OPEC+ policy that could impact crude prices.
  • **Cash Market Action for Livestock:** Further cash trade reports for cattle and hogs will be key to confirming the sustainability of the current price trends.
  • **Consumer Demand Trends:** Watch for further data or reports on consumer preferences for sugar and other commodities (e.g., orange juice, cotton) that could signal lasting demand changes.
