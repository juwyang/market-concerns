Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical Developments Drove Markets:** Hopes for a US-Iran peace deal were a major factor, weighing on crude oil prices and the US dollar while boosting stock market sentiment.
  • **Month-End Repositioning:** Widespread selling pressure was observed across grains and livestock as the month (May) came to a close, with money flowing out of positions.
  • **Weather and Supply Concerns:** Weather forecasts were a key driver for several softs, with above-average US temps supporting natural gas, fears of a weak monsoon in India lifting sugar, and dry conditions in Brazil pressuring coffee.
  • **Bullish Gold Narrative Weakens:** A key technical chart pattern (a "golden dome") on a gold mining ETF suggests the long-running bull rally in precious metals may have ended.
  • **Mixed Signals in Cotton:** Cotton saw mixed trade, caught between a supportive lower dollar and pressure from lower crude oil.

Risks Highlighted

  • **US-Iran Deal Collapse:** If the peace deal hopes fade, crude oil prices could spike and the dollar could strengthen, reversing the day's moves.
  • **India Monsoon Failure:** A weak monsoon in India poses a significant risk to sugar production, underpinning current price support.
  • **Abundant Cocoa and Coffee Supplies:** Rising inventories and improving harvest conditions in Brazil continue to pressure cocoa and coffee prices downward.
  • **Dollar Strength Reversal:** The dollar's decline was tied to Iran deal optimism; a failure of that narrative could lead to a sharp dollar rebound.

Watch Next

  • **US-Iran Peace Deal Developments:** Any news of progress or setbacks will be critical for crude oil, the dollar, and equity markets.
  • **India Monsoon Weather Updates:** Follow-up forecasts will determine the trajectory of sugar prices.
  • **Weekly Crop Progress Reports:** The grains sector is hitting a quarterly finish line, putting the spotlight again on real supply and demand fundamentals.
  • **Fed Policy Signals:** The dollar's direction could be influenced by any future central bank policy hints.
