US Dollar Index

-0.14%
Slipped. The dollar index (DXY00) fell by -0.14%.

Key Drivers

  • The dollar turned lower as T-note yields fell after a weekly ADP employment report showed the fewest/smallest number of new jobs, reducing expectations for hawkish Fed policy.

Reverse Factors

  • A stronger-than-expected upcoming employment report.
`Short-term influencer - DOWN`

Key Themes

  • **Geopolitical Supply Shocks:** Iranian attacks on Middle Eastern energy infrastructure provided a key catalyst, driving sharp rallies in crude oil, gasoline, and providing support to related commodities like natural gas and sugar.
  • **Agricultural Divergence:** While soybeans, corn, and livestock posted gains, wheat faced selling pressure and cocoa fell on improved weather. This highlights varied, commodity-specific fundamentals.
  • **Macro Data Influence:** A soft weekly ADP jobs report pressured Treasury yields and contributed to dollar weakness, demonstrating the market's sensitivity to labor market data.
  • **Soft Commodity Volatility:** Softs (Sugar, Cocoa, Coffee) were highly active, moving on a mix of energy linkages, weather updates, and supply concerns.

Risks Highlighted

  • Further escalation of conflict in the Middle East, disrupting energy supplies.
  • Changes in West African weather patterns affecting the cocoa crop outlook.
  • Continued supply disruptions in the coffee market.
  • Employment data significantly deviating from expectations, impacting Fed policy and the dollar.

Watch Next

  • Further developments regarding Iranian attacks and Middle Eastern energy supply.
  • Weather patterns in West Africa for the cocoa crop.
  • Updates on coffee supply chains for potential disruptions.
  • Subsequent employment reports for indications of labor market strength.
